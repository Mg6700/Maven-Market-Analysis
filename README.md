## Project Overview

This project analyzes Maven Market retail sales data across USA, Mexico, and Canada covering ₹1.76M in revenue with a 59.7% profit margin. The dashboard segments revenue by country, card type, gender, marital status, product recyclability, and low-fat product preference — enabling multi-dimensional customer and product analysis across a North American retail chain.

## Key Findings

**1. USA dominates at 66.76% of revenue despite serving 3 markets**
Two-thirds of all revenue originates from US stores — Mexico contributes a meaningful 27.14% but Canada (6.1%) is significantly underdeveloped relative to its market potential.

**2. 59.7% profit margin is exceptionally high for retail**
A profit margin of nearly 60% indicates either premium pricing, very low cost of goods, or high private-label product mix — significantly above typical retail margins of 15–30%.

**3. Bronze cardholders drive 55.91% of revenue — the entry-level card dominates**
Similar to the credit card analysis project, the base-tier card generates the most volume. Golden and Silver cards combined contribute only 21% — suggesting premium card upselling opportunity.

**4. Gender split is nearly equal: Male 49.46%, Female 50.54%**
Revenue is effectively gender-neutral — the product mix appeals equally across genders, which is unusual in retail where category mix typically skews one direction.

**5. Return rate is only 1% across 833K units sold**
An extremely low return rate suggests high product-market fit and customer satisfaction — or limited return policy accessibility across markets.

**6. Non-recyclable products (56.96%) slightly outsell recyclable ones (43.04%)**
Consumer preference still leans toward non-recyclable packaging despite growing sustainability awareness — a potential area for product strategy repositioning.
